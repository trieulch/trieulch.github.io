E-Commerce Analytics | End-to-End SQL Business Analysis

Overview

This project performs a comprehensive end-to-end analysis of an e-commerce business using MySQL, Python replicating real-world analytical workflows used by Data Analysts in technology companies.

The dataset contains realistic transactional, behavioral, and marketing data, enabling deep analysis across:

  • Traffic acquisition
  • Website performance
  • Conversion funnel optimization
  • Marketing channel performance
  • Product performance
  • User retention
  • Business growth and seasonality

The threshold constraint:

  • Conversion Rate: 4%

    • The goal is to answer stakeholder questions and generate insights to support marketing, product, and executive decision-making.

    Dataset Structure

    • website_sessions — traffic source, device, and session information
    • website_pageviews — user navigation behavior
    • orders — transaction records
    • order_items — product-level purchase details

    1. Traffic Analysis & Optimization

    Top Traffic Sources

    The 'gsearch' source and 'nonbrand' campaign contributes 97.4% of the total traffic, indicating that it is the most significant one.

    Conversion Rates by Top Traffic Source

    The conversion rate of the 'gsearch' source and 'nonbrand' is 2.96% (≤ 4%)

    Conversion Rate & Cost Per Conversion & Cost Per Action of top Traffic Source by Device type

    The conversion per action for desktop devices is significantly better at 53.62. Therefore, we propose reallocating more marketing budget to desktop, as it appears to be more efficient.

    Traffic Analysis for Product Launch Period

    However, the conversion rate threshold is 4%. Therefore, we decided to bid down on the desktop segment, which resulted in a avg 42.5% decrease in volume.
    The visualization presents a bid down from 'Pre' bid launch and 'Post' bid launch.
